Then, we will apply the leverage of your choice for that particular stock, and randomly determine the outcome of your investment decision. Thus, your payoff depends on the leverage that you choose and on chance.

Leverage

Leverage is a strategy that allows you to multiply our buying power to invest by creating a position with a larger exposure to the stock without investing more capital. Any amount of leverage increases your exposure to the stock. If you increase the level of leverage of your investment, it allows you to multiply your exposure to the stock in proportion to the leverage ratio (for example 5:1 as opposed to 2:1). The multiplier shows you how much buying power you will apply to your initial investment.

When you use leverage, your investment carries a higher degree of risk. Leverage increases the magnitude of both gains and losses. If you use leverage and chance moves against you, your payoff will be lower than if leverage had not been applied. If you use leverage and chance moves in your favor, your payoff will be higher than if leverage had not been applied.

For example:

If you invest USD 2 with no leverage, for every 1% move in chance you can gain or lose 2 cents, which equals 1% of USD 2.

If you invest the same USD 2 using 5:1 leverage, the dollar value of your position would be equal to USD 10. 1% of USD 10 equals 10 cents, so for every 1% move in chance, you can gain or lose 10 cents.
